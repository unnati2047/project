Heat Flow Analysis

Experiment Aim

The primary aim of this lab is to study how thermal energy moves through solid objects using mathematical modeling.

  • Quantify Thermal Conductivity: To measure the constant (K) which represents the material's ability to conduct heat.
  • Observe Temperature Gradients: To analyze how temperature decreases linearly across the length of a conductor under steady-state conditions.
  • Verify Fourier’s Law: To experimentally validate the relationship where the heat flow rate is proportional to the cross-sectional area and the temperature gradient:
    Q = -k * A * (dT / dx)
  • Compare Material Efficiency: To evaluate how different conductors (e.g., copper vs. steel) perform under the same thermal load.
